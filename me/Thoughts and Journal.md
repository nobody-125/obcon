### 2026-06-09
The interior of the SIIIs has been completed. I should feel accomplished and happy, but I feel empty and dissatisfied. I've become exhausted and disenchanted with this train project. I've been forcing myself to complete this piece of artwork. This is natural and expected as motivation and determination runs its cycle. But I think I had, in a way, made this project my entire life and purpose for the past few months. When I realized that it couldn't be everything for me, it felt disappointing.

I don't know how to explain the feeling, just this feeling of whatever I've done means nothing to me, I don't know where it comes from or why it is the way it is. I want to spend my life doing things I truly enjoy doing, I want to make artwork for myself and for other people, I want to build and create and make beautiful things. I'm getting exactly what I want right now, so why is it that I'm so unhappy? I have a small but close social circle. I'm not forced to interact with tons of strangers daily. I have a girlfriend whom I'm very happy with, and I'm not in school, but instead doing something which I should supposedly enjoy very much. Everything should make me happy, but instead I feel empty, disappointed, and jaded today.

Perhaps the answer cannot be found through logic, psychology, philosophy, or any of the other institutions of knowledge I would typically turn to for answers, my father would likely encourage a spiritual or religious explanation. As vague as such an explanation might be, it would explain a phenomenon that I could not explain on my own. It all feels like a miniature existential crisis right now. And I am afraid to talk about it with my parents, or most people in general. I would not want to conclude that the work and effort that I have invested to starting this path towards working on 3D design, 3D work, becoming an artist, has all been for nothing.

I am already aware of the flaws and bias within my own perspective. For one, I know that progress and purpose cannot always directly create happiness and fulfillment. An expectation that I will be happy every day in this life that I wanted is unreasonable. And as much as I may not believe it, I have other people I can turn to and talk to about this, even my own parents. Maybe it's the feeling that I haven't done enough on this particular project, that I should have made it more detailed, higher quality, that I should have been more passionate about it and put more effort into it. This is obviously impossible, as passion for something cannot be artifically increased.

Maybe things will become easier and better as I move on from the interior and to a new stage in the modeling process. The interior has been an excruciatingly difficult and tedious component to build and throughout it's entirety, I have felt unsatisfied with the process and the results of my effort. I am not going to remodel it; I think that the interior looks great and will suffice for the scope of this project. But I did not enjoy modeling this interior as much as I did enjoy modeling the underbody of the train, for instance. The number of interior faces that had to be constantly created and retopologized made this a particularly annoying process, in which lots of time had to be devoted to small details and corners which had little significance in the grand scheme of this project. I am in, to some degree, eager anticipation for how the textures and paint will turn out once I begin. I have dabbled with Substance Painter in the past, and it seems to be, to some degree, fun and engaging. I am also anticipating many difficulties with working with the program, but I hope that the result of the textured model will be worth the wait.

I've observed that as I write this, I'm being overwhelmed by the burden of everything else going on. The family situation, the fear and shame of me not making money or being in school, comparing myself to my brother and the path that he took being so different from mine. It's all too much to take in all at once. I wish that fulfillment came at a cheaper cost, that I knew how to be content with less. And while I would concede that some of my criticism of my own life is unwarranted, such concerns are unavoidable.

I think I need some time away from this city and house, too. I want to go on vacation sometime and just not think about any of this. But that will not be possible for the next few weeks. I also haven't been taking my medication regularly, which is definitely not good for my health. I hope that taking it a little more regularly will prevent these kinds of feelings and thoughts I'm undergoing right now. The future is bright, but maybe I am wearing sunglasses.
