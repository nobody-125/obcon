#### Benefits
Here are some takeaways from the initial schedule. It's available in this folder for archival purposes.
- **Consistency:** For the first time ever, I was able to stick to the schedule on a consistent basis. The schedule was put into practice in late February/early March, and I was able to stick to it for about 3-4 weeks in total. Almost every single day looked the same in terms of it's structure, and I managed to follow the schedule somewhat closely. I managed to wake up and go to sleep at somewhat regular intervals, however, sleep was somewhat disrupted.
- **Productivity:** Unprecedented levels of productivity were achieved during this period of time. Motivation levels remained highest between 12:00 and 16:00, with it fizzling out around 18:00. This was on $18$ $mg$ Concerta medication. In this period of time, I remained entirely focused on digital artwork and Blender.  
- **Mood:** I achieved a great amount of self-satisfaction during this time period. Although I did not necessarily look forward to the next day, I felt as if I had some kind of purpose for the first time, doing work that I felt was meaningful to myself. Getting such work done, on a consistent basis, was foreign. 
#### Shortcomings
- **Task-switching:** Eating remained difficult and somewhat inconsistent throughout this entire time period. Although I became more consistent and disciplined over time, it overall remained a difficult, conscious task that I had to go out of my way to do.
- **Sustainability:** This period of motivation was only sustainable for about a month. Subsequent of returning home, I have been unable to achieve this level of focus or replicate any of these behaviors once. There are several factors to this, but it has been over a month at home, and I still have not been able to replicate my previous level of discipline.
#### Conclusion
The first schedule, combined with medication, shows promise and illustrates what the self is capable of. The work and discipline from the "golden period" of the schedule remains strong proof of the existence of purpose and the benefits of a sort of delayed gratification. It has remained deeply de-motivating that I have struggled to replicate this discipline, however, these are extremely high standards for one to hold himself to.

Established is that there exists a desire to achieve or live up to this standard which I have now set for myself. Such a standard can be achieved iff there is something to motivate it. The ontogeny of so-called "motivation", however, is unclear, and inconsistent. Establishing goals with a tautological existence, which do not require a kind of motivation, would appear to be the logical next step.
#### Next Steps
Some of the ideas for the next schedule framework:
* Modularity in the framework, with some immutable aspects that add consistency.
* Greater precision, as the previous schedule had a *de facto* wake-up and curfew time. These times must be regulated as it's proven to be extremely difficult to keep with this in more recent times.
* More outdoors/physical activity: Not much justification is necessary for this, it's just healthy.
* Reintroduce the journaling, maybe make it's home here in Obsidian instead so that it's agnostic across both the Lenowo and the PC.
